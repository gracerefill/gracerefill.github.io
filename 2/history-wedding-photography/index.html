Born in the early 1840’s wedding photography has enjoyed a rich evolution. Throughout the 19th Century photography had little commercial use, however the idea of recording the memories of wedding days was born.

Like all innovations photography, and the practice of wedding photography, advanced over time. One of the first considered wedding photographs was taken at the wedding of Queen Victoria to Prince Albert.

This early period saw most couples considered a wedding photographer to be an unnecessary extravagance. If photographs were taken they were typically done so in a studio environment, dressed in their finest, a photograph of the couple was taken either before or after the wedding. This practice remained unchanged for more than a century.

The oldest known photograph was created by the French inventor, Joseph Nicephore Niepce around 1826-1827. The photograph was produced on a pewter plate. In the 1800’s photographic paper was yet to be invented, all photographs were exposed on a tiny copper sheet.

With the technological advances that emerged over the next century, not only did photographic paper emerge but, colour film and technology to create detailed imagery, this lead to the birth of a new vocation – the photographer.

The idea of capturing those moments in life that mean so much was born. Considering that the most momentous event in people’s lives is the celebration of their union it should come as little surprise that wedding photography became increasingly popular. Photographers would offer their services to the bride and groom, and for a negotiable fee these professionals would sell the photos to the happy couple. Despite the fact that photographic equipment was heavy, bulky and difficult to transport, an increasing amount of couples chose to capture the moment in time using the skills of a photographer.

The 1970’S saw traditional studio-based photography evolve once more. Location shooting was readily available. This dynamic new photographic style was coined as photojournalism, a process that allowed the story of your wedding day to be told through the pictures taken.

Today, photojournalism wedding photography remains the most frequently requested photography of couples. The advent of the digital age has lead to glamorous photographs of superbly intricate quality, together with a vast colour palette, being taken by skilled photographers all across the globe. Traditional pictures and wedding poses do still hold their place in a wedding album, however more often than not it’s the honesty and emotion of the day that couples request. Digital photojournalism is the future. We’ve come a long way since the 1840’s!
